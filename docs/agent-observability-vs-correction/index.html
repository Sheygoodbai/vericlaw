Observability explains the incident. Correction resolves it.

Teams searching for agent observability alternatives are often comparing visibility tooling against a workflow that can actually recover from hallucination, fake completion, and evidence-free closure.

VeriClaw belongs on the correction-first side: evidence, diagnosis, intervention, verification, and OpenClaw-native recovery loops.

Choose observability when

Observability is still valuable. The point is category clarity, not pretending one product replaces the other.

Observability-first fits

  • telemetry, traces, and alerting
  • fleet-wide visibility across many agents
  • incident debugging after execution

Correction-first fits

  • hallucination remediation
  • fake completion diagnosis
  • verification before human sign-off
