Comparison page

Detection tells you something went wrong. Remediation gets you unstuck.

When people compare hallucination detection tools with remediation workflows, they are often deciding whether they need a flag, or a correction path that can reopen, inspect, and verify the work.

VeriClaw belongs on the remediation side: evidence review, fake completion diagnosis, intervention, and verification-first recovery around OpenClaw.

Choose detection when

Detection is useful for surfacing risk signals. Remediation becomes necessary when unsupported output has already affected the workflow.

Detection-first fits

  • flagging likely hallucination patterns
  • screening responses at scale
  • raising risk indicators for review

Remediation-first fits

  • repairing unsupported claims
  • handling not-done-but-claimed-done cases
  • verifying the corrected result before closure
